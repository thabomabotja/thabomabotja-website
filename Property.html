Property

Real estate investing involves the purchase, management and sale or rental of real estate for profit. Someone who actively or passively invests in real estate is called a real estate enterpreneur or a real estate investor. Some investors actively develop, improve or renovate properties to make more money from them.

Some individuals and companies focus their investment strategy on purchasing properties that are in some stage of foreclosure. A property is considered in pre-foreclosure when the homeowner has defaulted on their mortage loan. Formal foreclosure processes vary by state and may be judicial or non-judicial, which affects the length of time the property is in pre-foreclosure phase. Once the formal foreclosure processes are underway, these properties can be purchased at a public sale, usually called a foreclosure auction or a sheriff's sale. If the property is not sold at the public aution, then the ownership of the property is returned to the lender. Properties at this phase are called REOs.

BRRR is a real estate investment strategy, used by real estate investors who have experience renovating or rehabbing properties to "flip" houses. BRRR is different from "flipping" houses. Flipping houses implies to buying a property and quickly selling it for a profit, with or without repairs. BRRR is a long-term investment strategy that involves renting out a property amd letting it appreciate in value before selling it. Renting a BRRR property provides a stable passive income source that is used to cover mortage payments while home price appreciation increases future capital gains.
